Sugar was the first good to be rationed in the United States because Japan's conquest of the Dutch East Indies in 1942 cut off the American supply of sugar from this region. As a result, the US government implemented a rationing system to ensure that sugar supplies were fairly distributed among the population. Rationing involved the use of coupons, which were used to purchase limited quantities of sugar and other goods. This measure aimed to prevent hoarding and ensure that essential goods were available to everyone during the war.
